# Research Report: Ergonomic Impact of Auto-Rickshaw Commuting
**Target Region:** Tier 2 & Tier 3 Cities in India
**Focus:** Back Pain & Spinal Health in Front-Seat Passengers

## 1. The "Front-Seat" Crisis
In many Indian cities, passengers often sit in the front cabin alongside the driver to save costs or during peak congestion. This study analyzes why this specific seating position is a leading cause of Musculoskeletal Disorders (MSDs).

## 2. Key Risk Factors
Based on ergonomic analysis, front-seat passengers face three primary health threats:
* **Twisted Posture:** Due to the driver's placement, passengers sit in a "sideways" or "perched" position, leading to asymmetric spinal loading.
* **Whole-Body Vibration (WBV):** The front seat is directly above the engine, causing vibration that stimulates resonance in the lumbar area.
* **Lack of Lumbar Support:** The front cabin provides no backrest, forcing the spine into a flexed position.
